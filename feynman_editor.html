Drawing tools (toolbar, or press the key)

Select QClick a vertex/line to select. Drag empty canvas = box-select. Drag a selection = move the group. Double-click a vertex/line to label it.

Fermion F · Photon P · Gluon G · Scalar S · Ghost H · Graviton TClick two vertices to connect them with that line type (graviton = double wavy line). Click the same vertex twice to make a self-loop.

Mouse

  • Double-click empty canvas → drop a new vertex. Double-click a vertex/line → edit its label.
  • Right-click + drag → pan (works in any tool).
  • With a line selected, drag its curve handle to bend it (use Reset curve in the inspector to straighten).

View

The + / 1:1 / − buttons (bottom-right of the canvas) zoom; press 0 to reset. Toggle Grid, Labels, and Snap-to-grid in the top bar.

Building the amplitude

  • iM ▶ — reads the Feynman rules off your diagram and writes iM, the momentum routing, the mass dimension, the loop measure ∫dᵈℓ, the UV degree of divergence, and the Lagrangian responsible (free + interaction terms).
  • dim d — the spacetime dimension. Changes the loop measure ∫dᵈℓ and the mass-dimension formula [M] = d − E(d−2)/2; the amplitude rebuilds live.
  • Copy LaTeX — copies iM as LaTeX. → Stepper — sends iM to the amplitude stepper to square it.

Inspector & Feynman rules (right panel)

  • Select a vertex/line to edit its label, style, type, and arrow direction; the matched Feynman rule is shown beneath.
  • A vertex label (e, q, Zf, lW, Hf, λ, 3g, …) picks the interaction. Gravity vertices: hff (graviton–fermion), hφφ (graviton–scalar), hAA (graviton–photon), hhh (triple graviton) — all from −(κ/2)hμνTμν. SMEFT baryon/lepton-number-violating vertices (BSM, dimension >4, contact terms ∝1/Λ or 1/Λ²): LLHH (dim-5 Weinberg operator, ΔL=2), duql/qque/qqql/duue (dim-6, ΔB=ΔL=1 four-fermion operators). Leave external-leg stubs blank — they auto-label p₁…p₄.
  • Feynman Rules ▾ — view/edit propagators, external legs, vertex rules, and the loop measure; Save rules or Reset defaults.
Tip: arrows show fermion-number flow (not momentum). Momentum & corner labels are positional: p₁=bottom-left, p₂=top-left (in), p₃=top-right, p₄=bottom-right (out).

Field declarations
Tell the parser which symbols are fields. Everything else is treated as a coupling or mass parameter.
shape: 2 → 2 (fixed)
Input notation
Kinetic scalar:(1/2)*(d phi)^2
Kinetic fermion:psib*(i*dslash-m)*psi
Field strength:-(1/4)*F^2 or F_mn
Mass term:(m^2/2)*phi^2
Power:phi^4 (= φ⁴)
φ⁴ vertex:-(lam/4!)*phi^4 → −iλ
φ³ vertex:-(g/3!)*phi^3 → −ig
QED vertex:-e*psib*gam^mu*psi*A
Complex scalar:declare phi (scalar) + phis (antiscalar φ*)
Scalar QED current:i*e*A_mu*(phis*(d_mu phi) - phi*(d_mu phis)) → real momentum-space rule, not just a note
Yukawa:-g*phi*psib*psi → −ig
4-fermion contact:(C/Lam^2)*d*u*Q*L (SMEFT-style, see "SMEFT B/L-violating" example → feeds Generate Diagrams)
Gamma matrix:gam^mu → γ^μ
Fraction:1/2, lam/4!, g/3!
Sign:- before a term flips vertex sign
Propagators come from 2-field terms.
Vertices come from 3+ field terms.
Identical-particle factorials are cancelled automatically
when a 1/n! coefficient matches n copies of a field.
